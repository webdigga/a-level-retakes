Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about A-Level retakes, GCSE retakes, and private tuition.

A-Level Retakes

Yes, you can retake your A-Levels. Students typically retake during the next available examination series - either in May/June or October/November. You can retake individual subjects or multiple A-Levels depending on your goals.
With dedicated study and expert tuition, significant grade improvements are achievable. In 2024, students achieved 80% A*-B grades and 43% A*-A grades through focused retake programmes.
A-Level retakes are available in two main examination series: May/June and October/November. The October/November series is popular for students who received their results in August and want to retake quickly.
Yes, most universities accept A-Level retakes. In fact, 77% of retake students secure places at Russell Group universities. Some competitive courses may have specific policies, so it's worth checking with your target universities.
Preparation time varies depending on your starting point and target grade. Some students prepare in a few months for the next exam series, while others take a full academic year for comprehensive preparation. A personalised study plan will be created based on your needs.

GCSE Retakes

Yes, there is no age limit for retaking GCSEs. Many adults retake English and Maths GCSEs to meet career requirements or to progress to further education. Private tuition can be tailored to fit around work commitments.
You can retake any GCSE subject. The most common retakes are English Language, English Literature, and Mathematics, as these are often required for further education and employment. Science subjects are also popular choices.
Yes, many students retake GCSEs in Maths or English while simultaneously starting their A-Level courses. Flexible timetabling allows you to fit GCSE retake preparation alongside your A-Level studies.

Private Tuition & Courses

Private tuition offers one-to-one personalised teaching tailored to your specific needs, pace, and learning style. Classroom courses involve small group teaching. Both approaches have proven results - the best choice depends on your learning preferences and goals.
Yes, flexible timetables are available to accommodate work, gap year activities, or other commitments. Private tuition sessions can be scheduled at times that suit you, and study plans can be adjusted to your availability.
A wide range of subjects are available including Sciences, Mathematics, English, Humanities, Languages, and the Arts. Courses can be tailored to any exam board and any combination of subjects is possible.
Simply make an enquiry and a specialist advisor will contact you to discuss your specific needs, goals, and the best options available to you.
