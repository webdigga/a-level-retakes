There are a wide variety of A level courses and A Level Retakes courses offered at Westminster Tutors Sixth Form College. They can teach all exam boards and any combination of subjects is possible, as their courses are individually designed and taught either one-to-one or in very small groups. A typical A level student would study three subjects over the course of two years, although they often admit ambitious students who would like to take a fourth A level subject or an EPQ (Extended Project Qualification) in Year 12.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What subjects are available for A-Level courses?
A wide range of subjects are available including Sciences (Biology, Chemistry, Physics), Mathematics, English, Humanities (History, Politics, Philosophy), Languages (French, German, Spanish, Latin), Social Sciences (Economics, Psychology, Sociology), and the Arts.

### Can I study any combination of subjects?
Yes, courses are individually designed so any combination of subjects is possible. This flexibility allows students to tailor their studies to their university and career goals.

### How many A-Levels should I study?
A typical A-Level student studies three subjects over two years. However, ambitious students can take a fourth A-Level or add an EPQ (Extended Project Qualification) to strengthen their university applications.

### What exam boards are covered?
All major exam boards are covered, including AQA, Edexcel, OCR, and WJEC. Courses can be tailored to your specific exam board requirements.

### Are courses taught one-to-one or in groups?
Both options are available. Courses can be taught one-to-one for fully personalised learning, or in very small groups. The approach is tailored to your learning preferences and goals.
