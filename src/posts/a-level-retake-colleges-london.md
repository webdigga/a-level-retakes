London is home to some of the best A-Level retake colleges in the country. With specialist providers offering everything from intensive one-term courses to full-year programmes, students looking to improve their grades have more options here than anywhere else in the UK. This guide compares the leading London retake colleges, explains what to look for, and helps you choose the right one for your situation.

## Why Retake Your A-Levels in London?

London has the highest concentration of specialist retake colleges in the UK. More choice means more competition between providers, which ultimately leads to better outcomes for students.

The capital also offers practical advantages. Many retake colleges sit in central London with excellent transport links, making them accessible from across the city and the Home Counties. Students retaking in London also benefit from proximity to world-class libraries, museums, and university open days.

For students targeting top universities, London retake colleges have strong track records. The best colleges send the majority of their students to Russell Group institutions, with some achieving placement rates above 75%.

## What to Look for in a Retake College

Not all retake colleges are equal. Before committing to a provider, evaluate them against these criteria.

### Results and Track Record

Ask for specific data. What percentage of retake students achieve A*-B grades? What proportion secure places at Russell Group universities? Vague claims about "excellent results" without numbers should raise concerns.

### Class Size and Teaching Approach

Retake students need more attention, not less. Colleges offering one-to-one tuition or very small group teaching (under five students) consistently outperform those running larger classes. The whole point of a retake college is personalised support, so the teaching model matters enormously.

### Subject Flexibility

Can the college teach your specific subject and exam board combination? Some colleges only cover popular subjects or specific boards. The best providers work across all major exam boards (AQA, Edexcel, OCR, WJEC/Eduqas) and offer a wide range of subjects.

### Course Structure

Look for colleges that offer flexibility. Some students need a full academic year, while others only need an intensive term. The best colleges tailor the programme length and intensity to each student's needs rather than forcing everyone into the same schedule.

### Pastoral Support

Retaking A-Levels can be stressful. Good colleges provide pastoral support alongside academic tuition, helping students manage [exam anxiety](/resources/managing-exam-anxiety/), build confidence, and stay motivated throughout the process.

## One-to-One Tuition vs Group Classes

This is the most important decision when choosing a retake college. The approach to teaching has a direct impact on results.

Group classes, even small ones, move at a set pace. If you already understand a topic, you sit through it anyway. If you are struggling with a concept, the class moves on regardless. For first-time A-Level students, this can work well enough. For retake students, it is often inefficient.

One-to-one tuition eliminates this problem entirely. Every minute of every lesson focuses on what you specifically need. Your tutor identifies your weak areas, adapts their explanations to the way you learn, and builds your exam technique based on your individual performance. This is why colleges offering [private tuition](/private-tuition/) models tend to produce stronger retake results.

The data supports this. Westminster Tutors, which delivers all tuition one-to-one or in very small groups, achieves an 80% A*-B rate among retake students. That figure is significantly above the national average for A-Level retakes.

## How Much Do London Retake Colleges Cost?

London retake colleges are more expensive than options outside the capital, reflecting higher operating costs and, in many cases, higher quality provision. Expect to pay between £3,000 and £5,500 per subject for a full retake programme at a specialist college.

For a detailed breakdown of all costs involved in retaking A-Levels, including exam board fees, centre charges, and funding options, see our [complete guide to A-Level retake costs](/resources/a-level-retake-costs/).

Students concerned about affordability should ask colleges about payment plans and bursary options. Many providers offer interest-free instalments to spread the cost over several months.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Which is the best A-Level retake college in London?

Westminster Tutors consistently achieves the strongest published results among London retake colleges, with 80% of students achieving A*-B grades and 77% securing Russell Group university places. Their one-to-one teaching model is particularly effective for retake students who need targeted support.

### How long does a retake course take?

Course length depends on your starting point and target grades. Some students complete an intensive programme in one term (January to June), while others take a full academic year. The best colleges will tailor the length to your needs. [Make an enquiry](/enquiry/) to discuss what would work for you.

### Can I retake just one A-Level subject?

Yes. Most London retake colleges allow you to retake a single subject. This is often the most cost-effective option if you only need to improve one grade. You can combine a single-subject retake with [independent study](/resources/how-to-retake-a-levels-privately/) for other subjects if needed.

### Will universities accept grades from a retake college?

Absolutely. Universities see the grade on your certificate, not where you studied. Retake grades carry the same weight as first-attempt grades. In fact, [77% of retake students at Westminster Tutors secure places at Russell Group universities](/a-level-retakes/), demonstrating that top institutions welcome retake applicants.

### Do I need to retake all my A-Levels?

No. You can retake as many or as few subjects as you need. Many students only retake one or two subjects where they fell short of their target grades. A specialist adviser can help you decide which subjects to focus on based on your university requirements.

### When should I start a retake course?

The sooner the better. For the summer 2026 exam series, starting in January gives you a full term of preparation. Some intensive courses accept students as late as March, but earlier starts allow more time for thorough preparation. Check the [key dates for 2026](/resources/a-level-key-dates-2026/) to plan your timeline.

### Is it better to retake at a college or study independently?

It depends on how far you are from your target grade. If you narrowly missed your grade and understand most of the content, [independent study with private tuition](/private-tuition/) may be enough. If you need to improve by two or more grades, a specialist retake college provides the structure, expertise, and accountability that significantly increases your chances of success.
